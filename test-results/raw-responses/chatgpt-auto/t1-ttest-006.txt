For a **two-arm, equal-allocation** phase III trial comparing **two means** (or a continuous endpoint) with standardized effect size **Cohenâ€™s d = 0.8**, the usual large-sample sample-size formula is:

[
n_{\text{per arm}} ;=; \frac{2,(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta})^2}{d^2}
]

**Given**

* Two-sided (\alpha = 0.001 \Rightarrow \alpha/2 = 0.0005)
* Power (= 99% \Rightarrow \beta = 0.01)
* (d = 0.8)

**Step 1: Critical values**

* (z_{1-\alpha/2} = z_{0.9995} \approx 3.2905)
* (z_{1-\beta} = z_{0.99} \approx 2.3263)

**Step 2: Plug in**
[
n = \frac{2,(3.2905 + 2.3263)^2}{0.8^2}
= \frac{2,(5.6168)^2}{0.64}
]

Compute:

* ((5.6168)^2 \approx 31.5484)
* (2 \times 31.5484 \approx 63.0969)
* (63.0969 / 0.64 \approx 98.59)

[
n_{\text{per arm}} \approx 98.59 ;\Rightarrow; \boxed{99 \text{ participants per arm}}
]

So you need **~99 per treatment arm** (about **198 total**), **before** any inflation for dropout/nonadherence (e.g., with 10% dropout: (99/0.9 \approx 110) per arm).
