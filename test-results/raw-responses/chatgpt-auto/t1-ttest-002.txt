You’re comparing **two independent groups** (new drug vs control) on mean cholesterol change, assuming equal SDs, using a **two-sided** test.

### Given

* Expected mean difference (treatment effect): (\Delta = 15) mg/dL
* Common SD: (\sigma = 30) mg/dL
* Two-sided (\alpha = 0.05 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2} = z_{0.975} \approx 1.96)
* Power (= 90% \Rightarrow \beta = 0.10 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta} = z_{0.90} \approx 1.2816)

### Sample size formula (equal n per group, two-sample mean comparison)

[
n ;=; \frac{2\sigma^2\left(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}\right)^2}{\Delta^2}
]

### Plug in

1. Sum the z’s:
   [
   z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta} = 1.96 + 1.2816 = 3.2416
   ]

2. Square:
   [
   (3.2416)^2 \approx 10.508
   ]

3. Compute the variance ratio:
   [
   \frac{\sigma^2}{\Delta^2}=\frac{30^2}{15^2}=\frac{900}{225}=4
   ]

4. Put it together:
   [
   n = 2 \times 10.508 \times 4 = 8 \times 10.508 \approx 84.06
   ]

### Result

* **Round up:** (\boxed{n = 85 \text{ participants per group}})

(Practical note: if you expect dropout, inflate: e.g., 10% dropout (\Rightarrow 85/0.9 \approx 95) per group.)
