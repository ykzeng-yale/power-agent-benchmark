For equal allocation (same n in vaccine and placebo), using the usual normal-approximation sample size for a one-sided test of two independent proportions:

Placebo infection rate: pC=0.10p_C = 0.10

Vaccine infection rate: pV=0.05p_V = 0.05

Effect: Δ=pC−pV=0.05\Delta = p_C - p_V = 0.05

One-sided α=0.025⇒z1−α≈1.96\alpha = 0.025 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha} \approx 1.96

Power =0.90⇒z1−β≈1.2816= 0.90 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta} \approx 1.2816

pˉ=(pC+pV)/2=0.075\bar p = (p_C+p_V)/2 = 0.075

A common planning formula is:
n  =  [z1−α2pˉ(1−pˉ)  +  z1−βpC(1−pC)+pV(1−pV)]2(pC−pV)2n \;=\; \frac{\left[z_{1-\alpha}\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} \;+\; z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{p_C(1-p_C)+p_V(1-p_V)}\right]^2}{(p_C-p_V)^2}
Plugging in:

2pˉ(1−pˉ)=2(0.075)(0.925)≈0.3725\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} = \sqrt{2(0.075)(0.925)} \approx 0.3725

pC(1−pC)+pV(1−pV)=0.10⋅0.90+0.05⋅0.95≈0.3708\sqrt{p_C(1-p_C)+p_V(1-p_V)} = \sqrt{0.10\cdot0.90 + 0.05\cdot0.95} \approx 0.3708

n≈(1.96⋅0.3725+1.2816⋅0.3708)20.052≈(0.730+0.475)20.0025≈1.4530.0025≈581.1n \approx \frac{(1.96\cdot0.3725 + 1.2816\cdot0.3708)^2}{0.05^2}
\approx \frac{(0.730 + 0.475)^2}{0.0025}
\approx \frac{1.453}{0.0025}
\approx 581.1
