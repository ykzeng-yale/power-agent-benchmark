For a Pearson chi-square test of association (independence) in an (r\times c) table, Cohen’s effect size (w) is linked to the **noncentrality parameter**:

[
\lambda = N w^2
]

and the test statistic under (H_1) is approximately

[
X^2 \sim \chi^2_{\text{noncentral}}(\text{df}, \lambda),
\quad \text{df}=(r-1)(c-1).
]

### 1) Degrees of freedom

For a (3\times 4) table:

[
\text{df}=(3-1)(4-1)=2\cdot 3=6.
]

### 2) Critical value for (\alpha=0.05)

Reject (H_0) if (X^2 > \chi^2_{1-\alpha,\text{df}}).

[
\chi^2_{0.95,6} \approx 12.592.
]

### 3) Power condition

You want power (=0.80):

[
\Pr\left(\chi^2_{\text{noncentral}}(6,\lambda) > 12.592\right)=0.80,
\quad \lambda = N(0.3)^2 = 0.09N.
]

Solving this yields (N \approx 151.38). Since (N) must be an integer and we need **at least** 0.80 power, check nearby integers:

* (N=151 \Rightarrow) power (\approx 0.7988) (just under)
* (N=152 \Rightarrow) power (\approx 0.8019) (meets target)

### ✅ Answer

[
\boxed{N = 152 \text{ total participants (minimum)}}
]

**Practical note:** the chi-square approximation works best when expected cell counts aren’t too small (rule of thumb: most (\ge 5)). If your margins are very imbalanced, you may need a larger (N).
