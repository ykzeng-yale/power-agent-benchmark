You can treat this as a **two-sample comparison of means** (equal allocation), with a **one-sided** superiority test.

### Given

* Expected mean difference: (\Delta = 0.4%)
* Common SD: (\sigma = 1.0%)
* One-sided (\alpha = 0.025)
* Power (= 95%) (\Rightarrow \beta = 0.05)

### Step 1: Get the critical z-values

* One-sided (\alpha=0.025 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha} = z_{0.975} \approx 1.96)
* Power (0.95 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta} = z_{0.95} \approx 1.645)

### Step 2: Use the standard sample size formula (per group)

For equal group sizes in a two-sample mean comparison:
[
n ;=; \frac{2\sigma^2\left(z_{1-\alpha}+z_{1-\beta}\right)^2}{\Delta^2}
]

Plug in:
[
n ;=; \frac{2(1.0^2)\left(1.96+1.645\right)^2}{0.4^2}
]

Compute pieces:

* (1.96+1.645 = 3.605)
* ((3.605)^2 \approx 12.996)
* (0.4^2 = 0.16)

So:
[
n ;=; \frac{2 \cdot 12.996}{0.16}
;=; \frac{25.992}{0.16}
;=; 162.45
]

### Result

* **Round up:** (\boxed{n = 163 \text{ patients per group}})
* Total sample size: (\boxed{326 \text{ patients}})

### Quick practical note

If you expect dropouts/non-adherence, inflate:

* 10% attrition: (163 / 0.90 \approx 182) per group
* 15% attrition: (163 / 0.85 \approx 192) per group
