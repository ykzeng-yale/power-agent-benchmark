You can treat this as a **two-sample comparison of means** (CBT vs waitlist), equal allocation, using the usual normal-approximation sample size formula:

[
n_{\text{per group}}
= \frac{2\sigma^2\left(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}\right)^2}{\Delta^2}
]

**Inputs**

* Expected mean difference: (\Delta = 8)
* Pooled SD: (\sigma = 12)
* Power (=90%\Rightarrow \beta=0.10 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.90}=1.2816)
* You said “more rigorous with (\alpha=0.01)” — by default this is interpreted as **two-sided**:
  [
  z_{1-\alpha/2}=z_{0.995}=2.5758
  ]

**Compute**

1. Standardized effect size:
   [
   d=\frac{\Delta}{\sigma}=\frac{8}{12}=0.6667
   ]

2. Plug into the formula:
   [
   n = 2\left(\frac{z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}}{d}\right)^2
   =2\left(\frac{2.5758+1.2816}{0.6667}\right)^2
   ]

3. Do the arithmetic:

* (2.5758+1.2816=3.8574)
* (3.8574 / 0.6667 \approx 5.7861)
* (5.7861^2 \approx 33.48)
* (2\times 33.48 \approx 66.96)

[
n \approx 66.96 \Rightarrow \boxed{67 \text{ participants per group}}
]
So total (N \approx 134).
