Using a standard two-sample comparison of proportions (two-sided α = 0.05, 80% power), with:

Standard success rate p1=0.85p_1 = 0.85

New technique success rate p2=0.95p_2 = 0.95

Difference Δ=0.10\Delta = 0.10

z1−α/2=1.96z_{1-\alpha/2} = 1.96, z1−β≈0.84z_{1-\beta} \approx 0.84

A common normal-approximation sample size formula gives:
n≈[z1−α/22pˉ(1−pˉ)+z1−βp1(1−p1)+p2(1−p2)]2(p1−p2)2n \approx \frac{\left[z_{1-\alpha/2}\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} + z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)+p_2(1-p_2)}\right]^2}{(p_1-p_2)^2}
with pˉ=(p1+p2)/2=0.90\bar p = (p_1+p_2)/2 = 0.90.
Plugging in yields:

n≈140n \approx 140 patients per group.

So you’ll want about 140 per arm (≈ 280 total).
If you expect dropouts/non-evaluable outcomes, inflate accordingly (e.g., +10% → about 156 per group).
