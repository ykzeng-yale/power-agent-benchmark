To use the **global shrinkage** requirement (Riley/Van Calster approach), a common approximation for a continuous-outcome linear model is:

* Likelihood ratio “signal” for the model:
  [
  \chi^2 \approx n ,\ln!\Big(\frac{1}{1-R^2}\Big)
  ]
* Global shrinkage factor:
  [
  S \approx 1-\frac{p}{\chi^2}
  ]

You want **S = 0.95** (≤5% shrinkage) with **p = 15** predictor parameters and anticipated **(R^2=0.15)**.

### Step 1: Convert the shrinkage target into a required (\chi^2)

[
S = 1-\frac{p}{\chi^2} = 0.95
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{p}{\chi^2}=0.05
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\chi^2=\frac{p}{0.05}=20p=20\times 15=300
]

### Step 2: Solve for (n) using the expected (R^2)

[
\chi^2 \approx n ,\ln!\Big(\frac{1}{1-R^2}\Big)
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
n \approx \frac{\chi^2}{\ln!\big(\frac{1}{1-R^2}\big)}
]

Compute the log term:
[
\ln!\Big(\frac{1}{1-0.15}\Big)
==============================

# \ln!\Big(\frac{1}{0.85}\Big)

\ln(1.17647)
\approx 0.16252
]

So:
[
n \approx \frac{300}{0.16252}\approx 1845.94
]

### Required sample size

**Round up:** **(n \approx 1846)** total observations.

**Answer: ~1,846 participants** to target shrinkage ≥ 0.95 with 15 predictors when (R^2 \approx 0.15).

*Notes:* This treats “15 predictors” as **15 fitted predictor parameters** (excluding the intercept) and assumes the anticipated (R^2) is the model’s apparent performance in the development sample under the usual linear-normal assumptions.
