Assume a **zero-inflated Poisson (ZIP)** with **30% structural zeros in both groups** (π = 0.30), and the remaining 70% are “susceptible” with Poisson means **λC = 3** (control) and **λT = 2** (treatment). You’re comparing groups at **two-sided α = 0.05** with **80% power**.

## 1) Convert to overall (marginal) means

ZIP mean:
[
\mu = (1-\pi)\lambda
]
So:

* Control: (\mu_C = 0.7 \times 3 = 2.1)
* Treatment: (\mu_T = 0.7 \times 2 = 1.4)

Difference:
[
\Delta = \mu_C - \mu_T = 0.7
]

## 2) ZIP variances

For ZIP with structural-zero probability (\pi) and Poisson mean (\lambda):
[
\mathrm{Var}(Y) = \mu + \frac{\pi}{1-\pi}\mu^2
]
(using (\mu=(1-\pi)\lambda)).

Compute:

**Control**

* (\mu_C = 2.1), (\mu_C^2 = 4.41), (\frac{\pi}{1-\pi}=\frac{0.3}{0.7}=0.4286)
  [
  \mathrm{Var}_C = 2.1 + 0.4286 \times 4.41 \approx 2.1 + 1.89 = 3.99
  ]

**Treatment**

* (\mu_T = 1.4), (\mu_T^2 = 1.96)
  [
  \mathrm{Var}_T = 1.4 + 0.4286 \times 1.96 \approx 1.4 + 0.84 = 2.24
  ]

## 3) Sample size for two-sample mean difference (normal approximation)

With equal (n) per group:
[
\mathrm{Var}(\bar Y_C - \bar Y_T) = \frac{\mathrm{Var}_C}{n} + \frac{\mathrm{Var}_T}{n}
= \frac{3.99+2.24}{n} = \frac{6.23}{n}
]

Critical values: (z_{0.975}=1.96), (z_{0.80}=0.842).
[
n = \frac{(z_{0.975}+z_{0.80})^2(\mathrm{Var}_C+\mathrm{Var}_T)}{\Delta^2}
]
[
n=\frac{(1.96+0.842)^2\cdot 6.23}{0.7^2}
=\frac{(2.802)^2\cdot 6.23}{0.49}
\approx \frac{7.85\cdot 6.23}{0.49}
\approx \frac{48.9}{0.49}
\approx 99.8
]

## Result

**≈ 100 subjects per group** (so **~200 total**) for 80% power at α = 0.05 under the ZIP assumptions above.

### Practical note

If the “susceptible” counts are **overdispersed beyond Poisson** (i.e., negative binomial among susceptibles), you’d inflate (n) roughly by the extra dispersion factor. For example, if that adds ~25% extra variance, you’d be closer to **125 per group**.
