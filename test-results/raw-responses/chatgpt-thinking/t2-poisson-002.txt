Assume infections follow a **Poisson process** with a constant rate, and each patient contributes **6 months** of exposure time.

* Control rate: (\lambda_0 = 0.5) infections/month
* Treatment rate: (\lambda_1 = 0.3) infections/month
* Follow-up per patient: (T=6) months
* Rate ratio: (RR=\lambda_1/\lambda_0 = 0.6)
* (\alpha=0.05) (two-sided) (\Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2}=1.96)
* Power (=0.80) (\Rightarrow z_{1-\beta}=0.84)

### Poisson rate comparison (Wald test on (\log(RR)))

For equal allocation, with (n) per group:

[
E_0 = \lambda_0 nT,\quad E_1 = \lambda_1 nT
]
[
\mathrm{Var}(\log \widehat{RR}) \approx \frac{1}{E_1}+\frac{1}{E_0}
= \frac{1}{nT}\left(\frac{1}{\lambda_1}+\frac{1}{\lambda_0}\right)
]

Power condition:

[
\frac{|\log(RR)|}{\sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(\log \widehat{RR})}}
\ge z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}
]

Solve for (n):

[
n \ge \frac{(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta})^2\left(\frac{1}{\lambda_1}+\frac{1}{\lambda_0}\right)}
{T,[\log(\lambda_1/\lambda_0)]^2}
]

Plug in values:

* ((1.96+0.84)^2 = 2.80^2 \approx 7.85)
* (\frac{1}{0.3}+\frac{1}{0.5}=3.333+2=5.333)
* (\log(0.6)=-0.5108), so ([\log(0.6)]^2 \approx 0.2609)

[
n \ge \frac{7.85 \times 5.333}{6 \times 0.2609}
\approx \frac{41.86}{1.565}
\approx 26.7
]

### Answer

**Round up: (\boxed{27 \text{ patients per group}})** (about **54 total**), assuming a Poisson model with no over-dispersion and complete 6-month follow-up.

If you expect **over-dispersion** (rates vary a lot between patients), a quick adjustment is to multiply (n) by the dispersion factor (e.g., 1.5× ⇒ ~41/group).
