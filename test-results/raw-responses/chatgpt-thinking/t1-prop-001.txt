For a **two-arm comparison of proportions** (control (p_1=0.40) vs treatment (p_2=0.60)), using the standard normal-approximation sample size for a **two-sided** test:

* (\alpha = 0.05 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2} = 1.96)
* Power (=0.80 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta} = 0.842)
* (\bar p = (p_1+p_2)/2 = 0.50)
* Difference (\Delta = p_2-p_1 = 0.20)

[
n ;=;\frac{\left[z_{1-\alpha/2}\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} ;+; z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)+p_2(1-p_2)}\right]^2}{\Delta^2}
]

Plugging in:

* (\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)}=\sqrt{2(0.5)(0.5)}=\sqrt{0.5}=0.7071)
* (\sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)+p_2(1-p_2)}=\sqrt{0.4(0.6)+0.6(0.4)}=\sqrt{0.48}=0.6928)

[
n=\frac{(1.96\cdot 0.7071 + 0.842\cdot 0.6928)^2}{0.2^2}
=\frac{(1.3859 + 0.5832)^2}{0.04}
=\frac{(1.9691)^2}{0.04}
\approx \frac{3.877}{0.04}
\approx 96.9
]

**Result:** round up → **97 patients per group** (≈ **194 total**).

Practical note: if you want a small cushion (or plan for dropouts / use a continuity correction), many teams would round to **~100 per group**.
