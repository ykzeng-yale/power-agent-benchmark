With 2 measurements (baseline and 8 weeks) and correlation ( \rho=\text{ICC}=0.6), the most efficient “simple” analysis is **ANCOVA**: compare 8-week outcomes between groups while adjusting for baseline. Baseline adjustment reduces the residual variance by a factor ((1-\rho^2)).

## 1) ANCOVA (recommended): follow-up adjusted for baseline

Let (d=\Delta/\sigma=0.4), where (\sigma) is the SD of the outcome at a single time point.

For a two-sample comparison with equal group sizes:
[
n_{\text{per group}}
====================

\frac{2,(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta})^2,(1-\rho^2)}{d^2}
]

Inputs:

* (\alpha=0.05) two-sided (\Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2}=z_{0.975}=1.96)
* Power (=0.80) (\Rightarrow z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.80}=0.84)
* (\rho=0.6 \Rightarrow 1-\rho^2 = 1-0.36=0.64)
* (d=0.4 \Rightarrow d^2=0.16)

Compute:
[
n = \frac{2(1.96+0.84)^2(0.64)}{0.16}
= \frac{2(2.80)^2(0.64)}{0.16}
= \frac{2(7.84)(0.64)}{0.16}
= \frac{10.0352}{0.16}
\approx 62.7
]

✅ **Answer (ANCOVA): ~63 subjects per group** (so **126 total**).

---

## 2) If you instead analyze *change scores* (less efficient)

Change score variance is ( \text{Var}(\Delta Y)=2\sigma^2(1-\rho)), giving:
[
n_{\text{per group}}
====================

\frac{2,(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta})^2,[2(1-\rho)]}{d^2}
]
Here (2(1-\rho)=2(0.4)=0.8):
[
n=\frac{2(2.80)^2(0.8)}{0.16}
=\frac{12.544}{0.16}
\approx 78.4
]
So **~79 per group** if you plan to test **change** directly.

---

## Practical note (dropout)

If you expect, say, 10% dropout:

* ANCOVA: (63/0.9 \approx 70) per group.

**Bottom line:** Plan for **~63 per group** (ANCOVA), or **~79 per group** if you’ll analyze change scores.
