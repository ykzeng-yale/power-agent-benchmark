To size a **two-arm vaccine efficacy trial** comparing **two proportions** (placebo vs vaccine) with a **one-sided superiority test**:

* Placebo infection rate: (p_C = 0.10)
* Vaccine infection rate: (p_V = 0.05)
* Difference to detect: (\Delta = p_C - p_V = 0.05)
* One-sided (\alpha = 0.025 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha} = z_{0.975} \approx 1.96)
* Power (= 90% \Rightarrow \beta = 0.10,; z_{1-\beta} = z_{0.90} \approx 1.2816)

A common planning formula for equal allocation is:
[
n ;=; \frac{\left[z_{1-\alpha}\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} ;+; z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{p_C(1-p_C)+p_V(1-p_V)}\right]^2}{(p_C-p_V)^2},
\quad \bar p=\frac{p_C+p_V}{2}
]

Compute components:

* (\bar p = (0.10+0.05)/2 = 0.075)
* (\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} = \sqrt{2(0.075)(0.925)} \approx 0.3725)
* (\sqrt{p_C(1-p_C)+p_V(1-p_V)} = \sqrt{0.10(0.90)+0.05(0.95)} \approx 0.3704)

Then:
[
n \approx \frac{\left[1.96(0.3725) + 1.2816(0.3704)\right]^2}{0.05^2}
\approx \frac{(0.7301+0.4745)^2}{0.0025}
\approx \frac{1.2046^2}{0.0025}
\approx 581.1
]

### Result

* **(n \approx 582) participants per group** (round up)
* **Total (\approx 1164) participants**
