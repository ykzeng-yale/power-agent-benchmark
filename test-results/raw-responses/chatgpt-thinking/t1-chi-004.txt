For a Pearson chi-square test of independence in a (4\times 4) table:

* Degrees of freedom: ((4-1)(4-1)=9)
* Cohenâ€™s effect size: (w=0.2)
* Test statistic under (H_1): (\chi^2_{df=9}(\lambda)) with noncentrality (\lambda = N w^2)
* Reject when (\chi^2 > \chi^2_{1-\alpha, df})

1. Critical value (one test, (\alpha=0.01))
   [
   \chi^2_{0.99,9} \approx 21.666
   ]

2. Power condition
   [
   \Pr\left(\chi^2_{9}(\lambda) > 21.666\right)=0.90,\quad \lambda = N(0.2)^2 = 0.04N
   ]

Solving this gives:
[
N \approx 653.07 ;;\Rightarrow;; \boxed{N = 654 \text{ total (round up)}}
]

So you need **about 654 total observations** in the (4\times 4) table to detect (w=0.2) with **90% power** at **(\alpha=0.01)**.
