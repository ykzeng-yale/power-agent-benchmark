For a Pearson chi-square test of independence in a **3×4** table:

* Degrees of freedom:
  [
  df=(3-1)(4-1)=6
  ]
* Cohen’s effect size (w) relates to the chi-square noncentrality parameter by:
  [
  \lambda = N w^2
  ]
  Here (w=0.3 \Rightarrow w^2=0.09), so (\lambda = 0.09N).

At (\alpha=0.05), the critical value is:
[
\chi^2_{0.95,6} \approx 12.592
]

Power is:
[
\text{Power} = P\left(\chi^2_{df=6,\ \lambda=Nw^2} > 12.592\right)
]

Solving for (N) such that power (\approx 0.80) gives:

* (N=151 \Rightarrow \text{power} \approx 0.799)
* **(N=152 \Rightarrow \text{power} \approx 0.802)**

✅ **Required total sample size: (\boxed{N \approx 152})** (round up to **152**).
