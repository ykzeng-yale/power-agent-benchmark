Treat this as a **two-sample comparison of means** (CBT vs waitlist), equal group sizes.

**Given**

* Target mean difference: (\Delta = 8)
* Pooled SD: (\sigma = 12)
* Power: (1-\beta = 0.90)  (\Rightarrow z_{1-\beta} = z_{0.90} = 1.2816)
* Significance: (\alpha = 0.01) (typically **two-sided** unless you explicitly want one-sided)
  (\Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2} = z_{0.995} = 2.5758)

**Sample size per group (normal approximation for two-sample t-test):**
[
n ;=; \frac{2\sigma^2,(z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta})^2}{\Delta^2}
]

Plug in:

* (\sigma^2 = 144)
* ((z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}) = 2.5758 + 1.2816 = 3.8574)
* ((3.8574)^2 \approx 14.88)

[
n \approx \frac{2 \cdot 144 \cdot 14.88}{8^2}
= \frac{4285.4}{64}
\approx 66.96
]

**Result:** (\boxed{67 \text{ participants per group}}) (so **134 total**).

If you expect attrition, inflate: e.g., **10% dropout** (\Rightarrow 67/0.9 \approx 75) per group.
