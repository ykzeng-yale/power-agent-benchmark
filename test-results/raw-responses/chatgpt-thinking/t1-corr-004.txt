To power a test of **Pearson correlation** (typically (H_0:\rho=0) vs (H_1:\rho\neq 0)) we usually use the **Fisher (z)-transform**:

[
z = \operatorname{atanh}(r)=\tfrac12\ln!\left(\frac{1+r}{1-r}\right), \qquad \text{SE}(z)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{n-3}}
]

For target correlation ( \rho_1 = 0.5), the “effect size” on the Fisher scale is:

[
\Delta = \operatorname{atanh}(0.5)=0.5493
]

Required sample size (approx.):

* **Two-sided test** at level (\alpha):
  [
  n \approx \left(\frac{z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}}{\Delta}\right)^2 + 3
  ]
* **One-sided test** at level (\alpha):
  [
  n \approx \left(\frac{z_{1-\alpha}+z_{1-\beta}}{\Delta}\right)^2 + 3
  ]

With **power = 95%** ((\beta=0.05)) and **(\alpha=0.01)**:

* (z_{1-\alpha/2}=z_{0.995}=2.5758)
* (z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.95}=1.6449)

### Result

* **Two-sided (\alpha=0.01):**
  [
  n \approx \left(\frac{2.5758+1.6449}{0.5493}\right)^2 + 3 \approx 62.0 \Rightarrow \boxed{63}
  ]
  ✅ **Need ~63 participants total**.

* **If you meant one-sided (\alpha=0.01):**
