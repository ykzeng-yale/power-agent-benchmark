Assuming you’re planning a **multiple linear regression** and the primary hypothesis is the **overall model test**
(;H_0: R^2=0;) vs (;H_1: R^2=0.05;) with **15 predictors**, **two-sided (\alpha=0.01)**, and **95% power**:

### Step 1) Convert (R^2) to Cohen’s (f^2)

[
f^2=\frac{R^2}{1-R^2}=\frac{0.05}{0.95}=0.05263
]

### Step 2) Use the overall F-test setup

Overall regression F-test has:

* numerator df: (u = 15)
* denominator df: (v = N-u-1 = N-16)

Power is computed from a **noncentral F** distribution using this (f^2).

### Result

The smallest total sample size that achieves **≥ 95% power** at **(\alpha=0.01)** is:

* **Total (N \approx 688)** (so (v=672))

So you need **about 688 participants total** for the *global* detection of (R^2 \approx 0.05) with 15 predictors.

### Practical adjustments

* **10% missing/attrition:** (688/0.90 \approx 765)
* **Clustered/complex sampling:** multiply by your **design effect** (e.g., DE=1.3 ⇒ (688\times1.3 \approx 894))
