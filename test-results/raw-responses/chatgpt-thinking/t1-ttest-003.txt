For a **two-sample comparison of means** (equal allocation) using a **one-sided** test, the usual planning formula in standardized units (Cohen’s *d*) is:

[
n_{\text{per arm}}
=\frac{2,(z_{1-\alpha}+z_{1-\beta})^2}{d^2}
]

Given:

* (d=0.3)
* one-sided (\alpha=0.05 \Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha}=z_{0.95}=1.6449)
* power (=0.80 \Rightarrow \beta=0.20 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.80}=0.8416)

Compute:

[
n = \frac{2,(1.6449+0.8416)^2}{0.3^2}
= \frac{2,(2.4865)^2}{0.09}
= \frac{2\cdot 6.1876}{0.09}
= \frac{12.3752}{0.09}
= 137.5
]

So you round up:

**✅ (n \approx 138) patients per arm** (total (\approx 276)).

If you expect dropout/noncompliance, inflate: e.g., 10% dropout → (138/0.9 \approx 154) per arm.
