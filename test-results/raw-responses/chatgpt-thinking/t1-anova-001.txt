For a **one-way ANOVA** (omnibus F-test) with **3 groups**, Cohen’s **f = 0.25**, **α = 0.05**, and **80% power**, we use:

* (df_1 = k-1 = 2)
* (df_2 = N-k)
* Noncentrality parameter: (\lambda = f^2 , N)

Power is:
[
\text{Power} = P\left(F_{df_1, df_2, \lambda} > F_{1-\alpha;, df_1, df_2}\right)
]
and we solve for the smallest (N) achieving power ≥ 0.80.

Numerically, the smallest **total** sample size that hits 0.80 is **(N=158)** (power ≈ 0.802), but that can’t be split evenly across 3 groups.

So with **equal group sizes**, round up to the next multiple of 3:

* **(N = 159) total ⇒ 53 per group**, giving power ≈ **0.805**.

✅ **Answer: 53 subjects per group** (total **159**).
