Using Riley et al.’s **minimum sample size criteria for developing a logistic prediction model**—(i) target global shrinkage ≥0.9, (ii) optimism in Nagelkerke’s (R^2) ≤ 0.05, and (iii) precise estimation of the overall outcome risk (margin of error ≤ 0.05)—we can compute the required (n). ([PMC][1])

## Step 1) Convert Nagelkerke (R^2) to Cox–Snell (R^2_{CS})

Riley’s formulas use **Cox–Snell (R^2_{CS})**, and Nagelkerke scales it by the maximum possible (R^2_{CS,\max}) (which depends on outcome prevalence). ([PMC][1])

With prevalence (\phi = 0.174):

[
R^2_{CS,\max} ;=; 1-\exp\left(2[\phi\ln\phi+(1-\phi)\ln(1-\phi)]\right)
]

[
R^2_{CS,\max} \approx 0.6032
]

Given anticipated Nagelkerke (R^2_N = 0.36):

[
R^2_{CS} = R^2_N \times R^2_{CS,\max} \approx 0.36\times 0.6032 = 0.2172
]

## Step 2) Criterion (i): shrinkage factor (S \ge 0.90)

Riley’s sample size equation (their Eq. 11) yields:

[
n ;\ge; \frac{p}{(1-S),{-\ln(1-R^2_{CS})}}
]
with (p=24,; S=0.90,; R^2_{CS}=0.2172). ([PMC][1])

Compute:

* (-\ln(1-0.2172)= -\ln(0.7828)\approx 0.2451)
* ((1-S)=0.10)

[
n \ge \frac{24}{0.10\times 0.2451} \approx 980.3
]

So **(n \approx 981)** participants.

## Step 3) Criterion (ii): optimism in Nagelkerke (R^2) ≤ 0.05

Riley recommend ensuring the **absolute difference between apparent and optimism-adjusted Nagelkerke (R^2)** is ≤ 0.05, which can imply a stricter shrinkage than 0.90 in some settings. ([PMC][1])
For your inputs ((R^2_N=0.36), (\phi=0.174)), this criterion implies a required shrinkage **< 0.90**, so **criterion (i) already satisfies criterion (ii)** (i.e., no increase beyond (n\approx 981)).

## Step 4) Criterion (iii): overall risk precision (MOE ≤ 0.05)

Riley’s Eq. (27) for a 95% CI margin of error (\delta) for the outcome proportion is: ([PMC][1])

[
n \ge \frac{1.96^2,\phi(1-\phi)}{\delta^2}
]
With (\delta=0.05), (\phi=0.174):

[
n \ge \frac{1.96^2 \times 0.174\times 0.826}{0.05^2}\approx 220.9
]

So criterion (iii) requires only **~221**, much smaller than criterion (i).

# Riley minimum required sample size

Take the maximum across criteria (i)–(iii): **(n \approx 981)** total participants. ([PMC][1])

* Expected events: (E = n\phi \approx 981\times 0.174 \approx 171)
* Events per parameter: (EPP \approx 171/24 \approx 7.1)
