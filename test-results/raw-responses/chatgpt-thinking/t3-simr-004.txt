Assuming **randomization is at the school level** (10 schools per arm) and outcomes follow a 3-level random-intercepts model

[
Y_{ijk}=\mu + u_i + v_{ij} + e_{ijk},
]
with variances (\sigma^2_{\text{school}}=0.05,\ \sigma^2_{\text{class}}=0.10,\ \sigma^2_e=0.85) (total (=1), so (d=0.3\Rightarrow \Delta=0.3) in raw units).

### 1) Variance of a **school mean**

Per school: (J=4) classrooms, (K=25) students each, so (N=JK=100).

The mean over all students in school (i) is
[
\bar Y_i = \mu + u_i + \frac{1}{J}\sum_{j=1}^J v_{ij} + \frac{1}{N}\sum_{j,k} e_{ijk}.
]

So
[
\mathrm{Var}(\bar Y_i)
= \sigma^2_{\text{school}} + \frac{\sigma^2_{\text{class}}}{J} + \frac{\sigma^2_e}{N}
=0.05+\frac{0.10}{4}+\frac{0.85}{100}
=0.0835.
]

### 2) SE for the **difference in arm means**

With (n_s=10) schools per arm,
[
\mathrm{Var}(\bar Y_T-\bar Y_C)= \frac{0.0835}{10}+\frac{0.0835}{10}= \frac{2(0.0835)}{10}=0.0167,
]
[
SE=\sqrt{0.0167}=0.1292.
]

### 3) Power (two-sided (\alpha=0.05))

Test at the school level with (df=2n_s-2=18).
[
\text{ncp}=\frac{\Delta}{SE}=\frac{0.3}{0.1292}=2.32.
]
Critical value: (t_{0.975,18}\approx 2.101).

Power (=P(|T_{18,\text{ncp}=2.32}|>2.101)\approx \mathbf{0.59}).

**Answer:** This design achieves **~59% power** at (\alpha=0.05) (two-sided), under the school-level randomization assumption.
