For a Pearson chi-square test of independence in a **2×2** table:

* Degrees of freedom: (df = 1)
* Cohen’s effect size: (w = 0.3)
* Significance level: (\alpha = 0.05) (right-tail chi-square critical value)
* Target power: (1-\beta = 0.80)

Key relationships:

1. Test statistic under (H_1) follows a **noncentral chi-square**:
   [
   X^2 \sim \chi^2_{df=1}(\lambda)
   ]
2. Noncentrality parameter:
   [
   \lambda = N w^2
   ]
3. Critical value:
   [
   c = \chi^2_{1-\alpha,,1} = 3.841
   ]
4. Power condition:
   [
   \Pr\left(\chi^2_{1}(\lambda) > c\right) = 0.80
   ]

Solving (\Pr(\chi^2_1(Nw^2) > 3.841)=0.80) gives the smallest total (N) of:

[
\boxed{N \approx 88 \text{ total}}
]

So you need **about 88 participants total** (e.g., if roughly balanced, think “about 44 per group” when the margins are close to even).
