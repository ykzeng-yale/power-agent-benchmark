To power a **test of Pearson correlation** (two-sided) at **α = 0.05** with **80% power** to detect a true correlation of **r = 0.30**, a standard approach uses the **Fisher z-transform**:

* Fisher transform:
  [
  z_r=\operatorname{atanh}(r)=\frac{1}{2}\ln\left(\frac{1+r}{1-r}\right)
  ]
* Required sample size (approx.):
  [
  n=\left(\frac{z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}}{z_r}\right)^2+3
  ]

Plug in your values:

* (z_{1-\alpha/2}=z_{0.975}=1.96)
* (z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.80}=0.842)
* (z_r=\operatorname{atanh}(0.30)=0.3095)

Compute:
[
n=\left(\frac{1.96+0.842}{0.3095}\right)^2+3
\approx (9.053)^2+3
\approx 81.95+3
\approx 84.95
]

**Round up ⇒ (n = 85) patients total** (since correlation uses one sample of paired measurements).

Practical tip: if you expect missing BP/BMI or exclusions, inflate: e.g., **10% attrition → 85 / 0.90 ≈ 95** patients.
