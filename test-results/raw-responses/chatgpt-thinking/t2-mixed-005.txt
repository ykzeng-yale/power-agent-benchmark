Assuming this is a **cluster-randomized design at the site level** (5 sites per arm, so 10 total), with a random-intercept model

[
Y_{s p r}=\mu+\tau\cdot \mathbb{1}(\text{treat})+u_s+v_{sp}+e_{spr},
]
where (\mathrm{Var}(u_s)=0.02), (\mathrm{Var}(v_{sp})=0.50), (\mathrm{Var}(e_{spr})=0.48) and total SD = 1, so the target mean difference is (\Delta = d\times 1 = 0.4).

### 1) Variance of a site mean

Each site has (n=15) patients, each with (m=3) repeated measures. The site mean averages over (n\times m) observations, so

[
\mathrm{Var}(\bar Y_s)= \sigma^2_{\text{site}}+\frac{\sigma^2_{\text{patient}}}{n}+\frac{\sigma^2_{\text{res}}}{nm}
=0.02+\frac{0.50}{15}+\frac{0.48}{45}
=0.064.
]

### 2) SE for the treatment contrast (difference in arm means)

With (J=5) sites per arm,

[
\mathrm{Var}(\hat\tau)= 2\cdot \frac{\mathrm{Var}(\bar Y_s)}{J}
=2\cdot \frac{0.064}{5}=0.0256,
\quad
SE=\sqrt{0.0256}=0.16.
]

So the noncentrality is
[
\lambda=\frac{\Delta}{SE}=\frac{0.4}{0.16}=2.5.
]

### 3) Power at (\alpha=0.05) (two-sided), using site-level df

With only 10 clusters, inference is typically based on **cluster df**: (df \approx 2J-2=8).
Critical value: (t_{0.975,8}\approx 2.306).

Power:
[
\Pr\left(|T_{df=8,;ncp=2.5}|>2.306\right)\approx 0.593.
]

## Power achieved

**≈ 0.59 (59%)** at **α = 0.05 (two-sided)**, under the assumptions above.

*Note:* If you (incorrectly for small #clusters) used a large-sample normal critical value instead of (t) with (df=8), you’d get a more optimistic power of about **0.71**.
