For two independent proportions (new vs standard), with **two-sided** (\alpha=0.05) and **80% power**:

* Standard success: (p_1 = 0.85)
* New success: (p_2 = 0.95)
* Difference: (\Delta = p_2-p_1 = 0.10)
* (\bar p = (p_1+p_2)/2 = 0.90)
* (z_{1-\alpha/2}=1.96), (z_{1-\beta}=0.84)

A common planning formula is:
[
n ;=; \frac{\left[z_{1-\alpha/2}\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)} ;+; z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)+p_2(1-p_2)}\right]^2}{(p_2-p_1)^2}
]

Plugging in:

* (\sqrt{2\bar p(1-\bar p)}=\sqrt{2(0.9)(0.1)}=\sqrt{0.18}=0.4243)
* (\sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)+p_2(1-p_2)}=\sqrt{0.85\cdot0.15+0.95\cdot0.05}=\sqrt{0.175}=0.4183)

[
n \approx \frac{(1.96\cdot0.4243 + 0.84\cdot0.4183)^2}{0.10^2}
= \frac{(0.8316 + 0.3514)^2}{0.01}
= \frac{1.183^2}{0.01}
\approx 140.1
]

**Answer:** **141 patients per group** (round up), so **282 total**.
