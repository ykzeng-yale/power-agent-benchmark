To power a **two-sided test of correlation** (H_0:\rho=0) vs (H_1:\rho\neq 0), a common approximation uses the **Fisher z-transform**:

1. Fisher z for the anticipated correlation
   [
   z_r=\operatorname{atanh}(r)=\tfrac12\ln\left(\frac{1+r}{1-r}\right)
   ]
   For (r=0.25):
   [
   z_r=\operatorname{atanh}(0.25)\approx 0.2554
   ]

2. Critical values

* (\alpha=0.05) (two-sided) (\Rightarrow z_{1-\alpha/2}=z_{0.975}\approx 1.96)
* Power (=0.90\Rightarrow \beta=0.10 \Rightarrow z_{1-\beta}=z_{0.90}\approx 1.2816)

3. Sample size formula
   [
   n \approx \left(\frac{z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{1-\beta}}{z_r}\right)^2 + 3
   ]

Plug in:
[
n \approx \left(\frac{1.96+1.2816}{0.2554}\right)^2 + 3
\approx 164.07
]

✅ **Required sample size: (\boxed{165}) participants** (rounding up).

*(If you expect missing data, inflate: e.g., 10% loss → (165/0.90\approx 184).)*
