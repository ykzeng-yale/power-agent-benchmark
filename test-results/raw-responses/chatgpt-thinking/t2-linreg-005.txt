To test whether **one specific predictor** adds meaningful explanatory power in a multiple regression (full model has **8 predictors**), the usual power calculation uses the **incremental (R^2)** / **partial (f^2)** framework for the **(R^2) increase F-test** (equivalently, the t-test for that coefficient when adding 1 predictor).

### Setup

* Tested predictors added: (u = 1)
* Full model predictors: (p = 8)
* Denominator df: (v = N - p - 1 = N - 9)
* Effect size given: partial (f^2 = 0.053)
* Significance: (\alpha = 0.05)
* Desired power: (1-\beta = 0.90)

For the (R^2)-increase test:

* Noncentrality parameter: (\lambda = f^2 \cdot v = 0.053,(N-9))
* Critical value: (F_{1-\alpha; u, v})
* Power: (\Pr\left(F_{u,v,\lambda} > F_{1-\alpha;u,v}\right))

### Result

Solving for the smallest (N) giving at least 90% power yields:

* **(N = 210)** total subjects (i.e., about **210 observations**)

(At (N=209), power is just under 0.90; at (N=210), it just exceeds 0.90.)

**Answer: (\boxed{210}) subjects total.**
